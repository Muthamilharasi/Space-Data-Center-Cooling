# Space-Data-Center-Cooling
Simulation-based project demonstrating space-based data center cooling using radiative heat transfer, eliminating the need for water and improving energy efficiency.
# 🚀 Space-Based Data Center Cooling using Radiative Heat Transfer

## 🌍 Problem
Modern data centers consume massive amounts of energy and water for cooling, leading to high operational costs and environmental impact.

## 💡 Proposed Solution
This project explores a futuristic approach — deploying data centers in space, where vacuum conditions allow heat dissipation through radiation, eliminating the need for water-based cooling.

## ⚙️ Simulation Details
- Tool: ANSYS (Transient Thermal- Engineering Data,Geometry,Mechanical Model,Setup,Solution,Results )
- Heat Source: 500W server module
- Cooling Mechanism: Radiative heat transfer
- Environment: Vacuum (no convection)

## 🔬 Key Physics Concepts
- Conduction (heat transfer within server)
- Radiation (heat loss to space)
- Emissivity (surface property of radiator)
- Stefan–Boltzmann Law
- Alluminum Alloy
- Silicon Chips

## 📊 Results
- Efficient heat dissipation achieved through radiator
- Stable temperature distribution observed
- No dependency on water or air cooling systems

## 🛰️ System Architecture
Server → Heat Conduction (Connector) → Radiator Panel → Radiation into Space

## 🌱 Impact
- Eliminates water usage in cooling
- Reduces energy consumption
- Supports sustainable computing infrastructure
